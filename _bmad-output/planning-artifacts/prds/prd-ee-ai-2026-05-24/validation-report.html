Adversarial review (Pass 2) — 2 critical, 5 high, 7 medium, 2 low

review-adversarial-general.md

The three first-pass criticals are genuinely closed at the spec level. That's real progress. But the fix cycle has redistributed risk rather than reduced it. The PRD grew ~50% (600→928 lines) while ostensibly removing scope. The v1.1 stack has hardened into a stack the solo author has never operated (Better Auth + Drizzle + Postgres + `@astrojs/node` + Stripe + systemd). Several "committed" thresholds (Lighthouse ≥95, WCAG 2.1 AA, 60s build budget, ~15-article v1.05 trigger) have been promoted from hopes to build-blocking gates without any baseline measurement.

Patterns flagged: (1) Author conflates spec-completeness with shippability — every fix added more spec. (2) Hard gates created faster than baselines measured. (3) Sequencing identified as the discipline, but the sequence is unenforced — the same solo author is the only person who would notice slip and act. The right next step is not another fix pass; it is a measurement pass.

critical

v1.05 corpus-size trigger will never naturally fire

§6.1.1, §6.3
At 1-2 articles/month from 5 articles, ~15 lands at month 5-10. But while the author is heads-down on v1.1 (months 1-9), cadence will decrease. v1.05 becomes the graveyard the first-pass review warned about.
Fix: Add a time-based OR-trigger. "v1.05 ships when (corpus ≥ 15) OR (12 months after v1 ships), whichever first." Without OR, "v1.05" is a politer word for "out of scope."
critical

9-month forcing function has no automatic mechanism

§6.3
"Automatically" is doing all the work; nothing makes it automatic. The same solo author 9 months into a slipping course build is the only person who would notice the 9-month mark, interrupt their work, evaluate options, and write off sunk cost. That's exactly the discipline this contingency exists to compensate for.
Fix: Pick one. (a) Pre-commit fallback — scaffold Lemon Squeezy account so switch is 2 days. (b) Hard calendar date in the PRD ("2026-12-01: re-decision day"). (c) Externalize trigger — accountability partner who pings on the date.
high

NFR-1/NFR-2 build-blocking with zero baseline

§7, §6.1
Lighthouse ≥95 and WCAG 2.1 AA never measured. First false-fail erodes the gate. The author's two options become: fix in minutes (best case) or "disable just this once" — which destroys NFR-6's credibility for the cookie-consent verification.
Fix: Measure first, commit second. NFR-1 should read "≥ measured-baseline OR ≥ 90, whichever lower for v1; ratchet up over time." 95 target lives in §8 as goal, not in §7 as gate.
high

FR-21 hard-gate spec contradicts static essay pages

§4.8 vs §4.7
FR-21 says "Server-side: each page render checks for the ee-consent cookie." But §4.7 says essay pages stay statically generated. A static page has no per-request render. FR-21 conflates SSR-route mechanism (correct for course routes) with static-route mechanism (impossible by definition).
Fix: Split FR-21 into two mechanisms. (a) Static essay pages: no GA4 tag ever in served HTML; inline script reads ee-consent and injects GA4 client-side if accept. (b) SSR routes: server-side check at render time. Both share the Playwright test suite.
