# Line Edit a Draft

Make the draft tighter and sharper without changing what the author is saying or how they sound. Work at the sentence and paragraph level, in the author's own voice.

## What to do

Read the whole draft first so edits serve the piece, not just the line. Then go through it surgically:

- **Cut.** Hedges ("I think maybe," "it could be argued"), throat-clearing intros, filler transitions, and points already made. The shortest version that keeps the meaning usually wins.
- **Tighten.** Replace flabby constructions — buried actors in passive voice, nominalizations, "the fact that," "in order to." Prefer the concrete noun and the active verb.
- **Fix rhythm.** Break up sentences that lost the reader; vary length so the prose has cadence. Read for where the ear stumbles.
- **Sharpen weak lines.** Where a sentence gestures at a point instead of landing it, show the version that lands. Where a claim is vague, push for the specific.

## How to deliver it

Quote the original line, show the edit beside it, and name the reason in a few words ("hedge — cut," "passive — name who acted," "two ideas — split"). Don't bury fixes in a rewritten blob the author has to diff by eye. Group trivial mechanical fixes; spend real attention on the handful of lines that carry the piece.

## The hard boundary

Edit what's there — never author what isn't. Don't invent examples, supply missing arguments, or write the paragraph the author left blank. If a passage is empty, hand-wavy, or asserts something the draft never earns, flag it for the author to write; do not fill it with generated prose. And edit toward the author's rhythm and diction, not a generic "correct" style — if the change erases the human, it's the wrong change.

## What success looks like

The author can accept or reject each edit at a glance, the draft is meaningfully shorter and sharper, and it still sounds like them — only better. End by naming the one or two structural or trust issues (if any) that a line edit can't fix, and point to the voice-and-trust pass or citation check for those.
